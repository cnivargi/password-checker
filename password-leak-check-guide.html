Why You Should Care About Password Leaks

In 2024 alone, over 5 billion passwords were leaked in data breaches worldwide. If you're using any of these passwords for your government portals, bank accounts, or email, you're at serious risk.

Here's the scary part: hackers don't need to hack YOU specifically. They just need to find your password in a leaked database and try it on popular sites. If you reuse passwords, they can access multiple accounts instantly.

What Are Data Breaches?

A data breach happens when hackers steal information from a company's database. This can include:

  • Usernames and email addresses
  • Passwords (sometimes encrypted, sometimes not)
  • Personal information (phone numbers, addresses)
  • Financial data (in severe cases)

Major breaches in recent years include LinkedIn (700M accounts), Facebook (530M accounts), and Yahoo (3 billion accounts). In India, several major platforms have been breached, exposing millions of users.

How Hackers Use Leaked Passwords

Once hackers have leaked passwords, they:

  1. Credential Stuffing: Try leaked passwords on other sites (banks, government portals)
  2. Password Dictionaries: Add them to lists used in hacking tools
  3. Targeted Attacks: Use leaked info to personalize phishing emails
  4. Account Takeovers: Access your accounts and lock you out

Common Myths About Password Leaks

Myth 1: "I'm not important enough to hack"

Reality: Hackers use automated tools. They don't target YOU specifically - they target everyone with leaked passwords.

Myth 2: "If my password was leaked, I'd know about it"

Reality: Companies often don't disclose breaches immediately. Some breaches remain unknown for years.

Myth 3: "Changing my password won't help if it's already leaked"

Reality: Changing passwords immediately stops hackers from accessing your accounts with the leaked credentials.

Myth 4: "Strong passwords can't be leaked"

Reality: Even strong passwords get leaked if the company storing them is breached. Always check.
